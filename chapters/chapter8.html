Chapter 8: Information Security and Cyber Law

Information security involves protecting information and information systems from unauthorized access, disclosure, modification, or destruction. Cyber law encompasses the legal aspects related to the internet and digital technologies, addressing issues such as cybercrimes and electronic commerce.

Information Security

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access.
  • Integrity: Safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods.
  • Availability: Ensuring that authorized users have access to information and associated assets when required.

Cyber Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity in electronic communications.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks: Attempts to make a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users by overwhelming it with traffic.

Cyber Law in Nepal

Nepal has implemented several legal frameworks to address cyber-related issues and enhance cybersecurity:

  • Electronic Transactions Act 2063 (2008): Provides a legal framework to regulate electronic transactions and prevent cybercrime. The act includes provisions for digital signatures, intellectual property, electronic records, and electronic commerce.
