8.1 Digital Society and Computer Ethics

The digital society is an interconnected environment where individuals and organizations use digital technologies for communication, business, and daily activities. Computer ethics involve guidelines and principles that ensure responsible and ethical use of technology, preventing misuse and promoting fairness.

8.2 Concept of Information Security

Information security refers to the protection of digital data from unauthorized access, breaches, and cyber threats. It involves measures such as encryption, firewalls, authentication, and access controls to safeguard sensitive information.

8.3 Concept of Cybercrime

Cybercrime includes illegal activities carried out using digital devices and the internet. Examples include hacking, identity theft, online fraud, and cyberbullying. Governments and organizations implement cybersecurity measures to combat cybercrime.

8.4 Malicious Software and Spam

Malicious software, or malware, includes viruses, worms, trojans, and ransomware designed to harm or exploit systems. Spam refers to unwanted and unsolicited emails that can contain phishing links or malware attachments.

8.5 Protection from Cybercrime

To protect against cybercrime, individuals and organizations should use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, regularly update software, and be cautious about online threats. Cyber awareness and training also play a vital role in defense against cybercrime.

8.6 Intellectual Property Rights

Intellectual property rights (IPR) protect creators’ works, such as software, music, books, and inventions, from unauthorized use. Copyright, patents, and trademarks ensure that individuals and companies maintain ownership of their creations.

8.7 Concept of Digital Signature

A digital signature is an electronic method of verifying the authenticity and integrity of digital documents. It uses encryption techniques to ensure that the sender’s identity is genuine and that the document has not been altered.

8.8 Concept of Cyber Law in Nepal

Cyber law in Nepal governs the legal aspects of online activities, covering issues such as digital transactions, cybercrime, and data protection. The Electronic Transaction Act (ETA) of Nepal addresses cyber-related offenses and enforces legal actions against violators.

8.9 ICT Policy in Nepal

The Information and Communication Technology (ICT) Policy in Nepal aims to promote digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, and technological advancements in the country. It focuses on digital inclusion, e-governance, and innovation in the ICT sector.
